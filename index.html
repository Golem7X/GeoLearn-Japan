рҹ’Ў How to Read an SPT Log
1
Read the N-value trend
Low N (1вҖ“4) = very soft/loose. Increasing N with depth is normal. A sudden jump (e.g. N=5вҶ’45) often indicates a layer boundary or bedrock approach.
2
Check the groundwater depth
Noted as GWL on logs. Critical for effective stress calculations and liquefaction assessment. In Japan, GWL is commonly 1вҖ“3m below surface.
3
Correlate with soil description
N-value alone is not enough. Sandy layers with N<10 below GWL are liquefiable candidates. Clayey layers are assessed for consolidation settlement.
4
Engineering decisions from N
Foundation depth, pile tip depth, liquefaction risk zones, and ground improvement requirements all flow directly from the SPT log interpretation.

рҹ”Қ CPTu Reading Guide
qc
Tip resistance
qc high (>5 MPa) = dense sand/gravel. qc low (0.5вҖ“2 MPa) = soft clay or loose sand. Very high (>15 MPa) = gravel or weathered rock. Low in clay = undrained behavior.
fs
Sleeve friction
fs high relative to qc (Fr>4%) = fine-grained (clay/silt). fs low (Fr<1%) = coarse-grained (sand). The friction ratio Fr = fs/qt distinguishes soil types.
u2
Pore pressure response
u2 > u0 = positive excess вҶ’ clay or constrained dilation. u2 < u0 = negative excess вҶ’ dense sand dilates. Negative u2 = strong dense material. Used to identify drainage conditions.
Ic
Soil Behavior Type Index
Ic = вҲҡ((3.47вҲ’logQt)ВІ+(1.22+logFr)ВІ). Ic<2.05: sands. 2.05вҖ“2.95: silts. >2.95: clays. Automated classification without soil samples.
рҹ§® CPTu вҶ’ N-value Conversion
N вүҲ qc / (0.02 + 0.002В·FrВІ) Г— (1/Pa)^0.5
Approximate correlation (Robertson & Cabal). Use with caution вҖ” regional calibration recommended.

рҹ”Қ Interpreting PS Logs
1
Read velocity trends
Vs generally increases with depth (compaction, stress). Soft layer: Vs < 150 m/s. Dense: 200вҖ“400. Rock: >700. A sudden increase indicates bedrock or gravel layer.
2
Check Vp/Vs ratio
Above water table: Vp/Vs вүҲ 1.5вҖ“2.5. Below water table: Vp jumps to ~1500 m/s (water velocity) while Vs stays low. Vp/Vs > 4 = saturated soft soil.
3
Calculate GвӮҖ per layer
GвӮҖ = ПҒВ·VsВІ. This is the small-strain (dynamic) shear modulus. Stiffer soil вҶ’ higher Vs вҶ’ higher GвӮҖ. Used for seismic response analysis and foundation stiffness.
4
Compute Vs30 for site class
Vs30 = 30/ОЈ(hi/Vsi). Compare to Japanese е‘ҠзӨә1457еҸ· and NEHRP. Vs30 <150 m/s = з¬¬3зЁ®ең°зӣӨ (very soft) вҶ’ strong amplification of seismic waves.

рҹ”Қ Reading the Pressuremeter Curve
I
Phase I вҖ” Re-inflation (elastic)
Initial straight-line portion as probe re-compresses disturbed zone. Slope gives pressuremeter modulus Em. This zone represents KвӮҖ in-situ horizontal stress state.

Linear elastic response. The key phase for computing Em = 2(1+ОҪ)В·VВ·(О”P/О”V). This modulus is used directly in settlement calculation and foundation design.

Non-linear response approaching soil failure. Limit pressure PL is asymptotically approached. PL correlates to ultimate bearing capacity and pile shaft resistance.

Lateral spreading occurs when liquefied soil flows toward a free face вҖ” riverbank or slope. Ground cracks open perpendicular to the flow direction, and structures spanning the zone are torn apart.
вҡҷпёҸEngineers use the Youd et al. empirical model to predict lateral spread displacement from M, R, and NвӮҒвӮҚвӮҶвӮҖвӮҺ values.

Sand boils (з ӮеҷҙеҮә) are the surface expression of liquefaction. Water and sand erupt from cracks as excess pore pressure dissipates upward. They confirm that Пғ'вҶ’0 occurred at depth during shaking.
вҡҷпёҸThe presence of sand boils post-earthquake immediately flags an area for FL assessment and ground improvement before reconstruction.
// BOREHOLE & FIELD INVESTIGATION
рҹ“Қ Field Site
SPT Borehole Drilling вҖ” гғңгғјгғӘгғігӮ°иӘҝжҹ»
The Standard Penetration Test (SPT) remains Japan's most common ground investigation method. A 63.5kg hammer drops 760mm to drive a split-spoon sampler 300mm. The N-value (blow count) characterizes soil density and strength.
вҡҷпёҸJapan's design codes directly use N-value for liquefaction screening, bearing capacity of spread footings, and pile tip resistance estimation.
рҹ“Қ Laboratory
Undisturbed Soil Core Sampling
Thin-walled Shelby tube samples (Osterberg sampler) preserve clay fabric for consolidation and strength tests. Sample quality is assessed by void ratio change and stress history inspection before testing.
вҡҷпёҸSample disturbance is the #1 source of error in soft clay strength testing вҖ” proper handling, wax sealing, and cold storage are essential from field to lab.
// SEISMIC SURVEYS & GEOPHYSICS
рҹ“Қ Survey Line
Surface Wave Survey вҖ” MASW / иЎЁйқўжіўжҺўжҹ»
MASW (Multichannel Analysis of Surface Waves) deploys geophones in a linear array to measure Rayleigh wave dispersion. Inversion yields a continuous Vs profile to 30m depth without drilling.
вҡҷпёҸMASW is especially valuable in Japan for rapid Vs30 mapping of large construction sites before borehole locations are decided, reducing total investigation cost.
рҹ“Қ Rock Formation

Rock Quality Designation (RQD) is measured directly from drill core: sum of pieces вүҘ100mm divided by total core run length Г— 100%. It's the fastest field indicator of rock mass quality for foundation and tunnel design.
вҡҷпёҸRQD 90вҖ“100% = Excellent rock. Below 25% = Very Poor. Japanese tunneling practice uses RQD to select support pattern and face advance distance.
// FOUNDATIONS & CONSTRUCTION
рҹ“Қ Urban Construction

Bored cast-in-place piles are the dominant deep foundation type in Japanese urban construction. They pass through soft alluvial layers to reach bearing strata, transferring structural loads through both tip resistance and shaft friction.
вҡҷпёҸJapanese Building Standard Law requires proof loading or integrity testing of at least 1% of piles per project. Ps-logging and cross-hole sonic are standard QA methods.
